# Devlog — week 2: calibrated verdicts

2026-07-12

## What shipped

Week 2 was about the word the one-liner stakes everything on:
*calibrated*. At the start of the week `/v1/verify` returned a raw model
score for the whole answer; now the answer is decomposed into sentence
claims, scored in a single joint pass against all passages, unsupported
regions come back as spans, and every confidence in the response is a
calibrated probability. The sequencing held as planned: the tier-2
response-shape ADR first, then its implementation, then the calibration
methodology and its implementation, so calibration locked onto the
scoring mode that would actually ship (ADR-0007, ADR-0008, ADR-0009).

Around the core: a CLA gate before outside contributions, a structural
realignment that brought `engine/` and `evals/bench` back in line with
the documented architecture before drift compounded, and the first real
measurement of fast mode's latency contract — more on that below.

## The tier-2 shape

A claim is a verbatim sentence of the answer, nothing more — no
rewriting, no LLM in the decomposition path (ADR-0009). The whole answer
is scored in one joint forward pass against the union of the provided
passages, and the token-level output is segmented into per-sentence
scores afterwards. Segment-after-score is the load-bearing trick: the
model sees the full answer, so a sentence like "It has a population of
50 million people." is scored with its antecedent present, while the
response still attributes the problem to the sentence it lives in. The
cost is that the claim *text* stays decontextualized — a dangling
pronoun read alone — which is harmless until retrieval runs per claim.
That constraint is parked in #47 so the retrieval ADR inherits it
instead of rediscovering it.

Spans localize, they don't judge: an unsupported region inside a claim
carries its own calibrated confidence and its own flagging threshold,
separate from the verdict threshold (ADR-0007). And decomposition
refined attribution without moving the gate — the whole-answer verdict
equals the conjunction of the per-claim verdicts at the same threshold.
The API broke once for all of it: `evidence_index` out, `spans` in.

## The calibration decisions

Platt scaling, fitted on the RAGTruth test responses held out from the
benchmark slice, so nothing the calibrator saw evaluates it (ADR-0008).
The resulting `confidence` means what it says: among claims the engine
scores `c`, about a fraction `c` are fully supported. Out-of-domain
reliability is measured on LLM-AggreFact and published next to the
in-domain numbers in `evals/RESULTS.md` rather than left as a footnote.

The artifact is a versioned file in `config/calibration/`, bound to the
exact model revision, inference mode, claim unit, and span-flagging
threshold it was fitted against. A mismatch doesn't degrade to a raw
score — the engine refuses to start. Serving an uncalibrated number
while claiming calibration would be the one lie this product cannot
afford.

## The number that missed

ADR-0003 gives fast mode a sub-second p95 contract and calls its numbers
targets until measured. Measured end to end — HTTP to verdict, on the
laptop-CPU protocol — fast mode sits at 340 ms median and **1,198 ms
p95**: the contract is missed at the tail by ~20%. The serve path adds
~125 ms at the median over scorer-only time and roughly doubles the
tail, where long contexts already make the forward pass expensive. The
number is published in `evals/RESULTS.md` as a gap to close, not a pass
to defend.

## Next week

Three threads. Retrieval gets its ADR — per-claim retrieval and thorough
mode, designed against the coreference constraint #47 carries forward.
The p95 gap gets closed rather than renegotiated; the tail lives in long
contexts and the serve-path overhead, both of which have obvious first
moves. And the NLI signal enters the stack, bringing `contradicted` into
the verdict vocabulary — the first time two signals have to be
aggregated, which is what the aggregation layer has been waiting for.
